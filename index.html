About Me

My work is deeply influenced by the organic beauty and subtle complexity found in nature. Each sculpture is a dialogue between form, material, and space, where clay becomes a medium for interpreting the natural world. The tactile essence of clay allows me to explore the textures, shapes, and rhythms of the landscapes that surround us—from the delicate structures of fungi to the raw power of rock formations. In my hands, these natural elements evolve into abstract forms, pushing the boundaries of what nature creates and what we imagine. I seek to bridge the gap between functional art and sculpture, using ceramics to craft pieces that are as evocative as they are meaningful. My work is a reflection on the deep connection between human experience and the organic forms that shape our environment. Through each piece, I aim to convey a sense of earthiness, delicacy, and timelessness, celebrating the fluidity of nature and the role it plays in shaping our collective existence.
